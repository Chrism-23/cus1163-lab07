1. Compare the results with time quantum 3 and time quantum 5. Which quantum gave better average waiting time? Why?
	Time quantum 5 gave better average waiting time because it didn't need to switch between processes as much and as such experienced less overhead from context switches.

2. What would happen if the time quantum was 1ms? Would the system be more or less efficient?
	If the time quantum was 1ms, the system would be less efficient due to increased overhead from context switching.

3. What would happen if the time quantum was 20ms (larger than any process burst time)? How would Round Robin behave then?
	If the time quantum was 20ms, then every process would complete within 1 time quantum. This causes Round Robin to behave just like FCFS.

4. In the simulation with quantum 3, how many context switches occurred? Count each time a process was removed from the queue.
	In the simulation with with quantum 3, 6 context switches occur. They happen in 1. P1 (0-3ms), 2. P2 (3-6ms), 3. P3 (6-8ms), 4. P1 (8-11ms), 5. P2 (11-12ms), and 6. P1 (12-13ms)

5. Why does Round Robin prevent the convoy effect that occurs in FCFS scheduling?
	Round Robin prevents the convoy effect that occurs in FCFS scheduling because each process only has a certain amount of time to execute. If a process is too long, then it will be interrupted and other process are given time to execute. In comparison, in FCFS, if a process takes a long time, then it will execute to completion with other processes forced to wait until completion.
